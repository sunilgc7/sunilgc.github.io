Biography

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate collaborating with USDA and NDSU researchers, focusing on applying computer vision and AI to quantify frost damage in crops, including strawberries, camelina, and canola. I have experience working with ground robotics, hyperspectral and multispectral sensors, cloud-based data acquisition systems, and IoT technology. My expertise lies in the field of precision agriculture, with a multidisciplinary background in electronics, communication, and agricultural engineering. My focus areas include drone technology, AI, robotics, software, precision weed management, and plant stress detection and quantification.

I have also worked as an embedded software engineer in the precision agriculture industry and have developed computer vision and AI pipelines for agricultural applications, publishing research in these areas. I’m involved in open-source precision agriculture software development focused on AI and computer vision.
